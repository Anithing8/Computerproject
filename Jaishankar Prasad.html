Jaishankar Prasad

Jaishankar Prasad was a prominent figure in modern Hindi literature as well as Hindi theatre. He was also known as a Chhayavadi poet, one of the four pillars of romanticism in Hindi literature. He wrote poems, dramas, stories, novels, and essays that reflect his versatile genius and humanistic vision

Prasad was born on 30 January 1889 in Varanasi, India. He belonged to a wealthy and influential family that was involved in social and religious reforms. He started writing poetry with the pen name of ‘Kaladhar’. His first collection of poems, Chitradhar, was written in Braj dialect of Hindi but his later works were written in Khadi dialect or Sanskritized Hindi. He also founded Chhayavad, a literary trend in Hindi literature

Prasad wrote many famous works such as Kamayani (an epic about Manu and the flood), Skandagupta (a historical drama about Emperor Skandagupta), Kankal (a novel about social issues), and Aansu (a collection of poems). His works are known for their lyrical beauty, emotional depth, and realistic portrayal of the lives of the poor and oppressed classes. He was influenced by Hindi, Urdu, Persian, Sanskrit, and English languages and literature. He also participated in the Indian independence movement and supported Gandhi’s non-violence

Prasad died on 15 November 1937 in Varanasi at the age of 47. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest literary figures of India and the world. His works have been translated into many languages and have inspired generations of readers and writers. He received many awards and honors for his literary contributions, such as the Sahitya Akademi Award, the Padma Shri, and the Bharat Ratna (posthumously)
