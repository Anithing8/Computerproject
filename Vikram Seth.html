Vikram Seth

Vikram Seth is an Indian poet, novelist, and travel writer who has written several novels and poetry books in English. He has won several awards such as Padma Shri, Sahitya Academy Award, Pravasi Bharatiya Samman, WH Smith Literary Award and Crossword Book Award. He is best known for his verse novel The Golden Gate and his epic novel A Suitable Boy

Seth was born on 20 June 1952 in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India. His father was an executive of Bata Shoes and his mother was a judge and the first woman to become Chief Justice of a state High Court in India. He studied at The Doon School in Dehradun, Tonbridge School in England, Corpus Christi College in Oxford, Stanford University in the U.S., and Nanjing University in China. He has travelled widely and lived in Britain, California, India and China

Seth’s first book of poetry, Mappings, was published in 1980. His first novel, The Golden Gate, was published in 1986 and was written entirely in metred, rhyming 14-line stanzas based on Aleksandr Pushkin’s Eugene Onegin. His second novel, A Suitable Boy, was published in 1993 and was one of the longest novels ever written in English. It depicts the relations between four Indian families in the post-independence era. His other works include An Equal Music, a novel about a violinist’s love life; Two Lives, a memoir of his great-uncle and aunt; The Rivered Earth, a collection of poems and essays; and A Suitable Girl, a sequel to A Suitable Boy

Seth is widely regarded as one of the greatest literary figures of India and the world. His works have been translated into many languages and have inspired generations of readers and writers. He is also revered as a national icon and a global citizen who enriched the cultural heritage of humanity
