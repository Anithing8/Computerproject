Rahi Masoom Raza

Rahi Masoom Raza was an Indian Urdu and Hindi poet, writer, and a Bollywood lyricist. He was also known as the ‘Soldier of the Pen’ and the ‘Emperor of Novels’. He wrote many poems, novels, stories, essays, and songs that reflect his versatile genius and humanistic vision. He also wrote the script and dialogues for the popular TV serial Mahabharat based on the epic of the same name

Raza was born on 1 September 1927 in Gangauli, a village in Ghazipur district of Uttar Pradesh. He belonged to a Muslim family that was involved in social and religious reforms. He completed his early education in Ghazipur and then went to Aligarh Muslim University to complete his higher studies. He obtained his doctorate in Hindustani Literature and pursued a career in literature. He later moved to Bombay (now Mumbai) and became a successful screenplay writer and wrote the screenplays and dialogues for over 300 films

Some of his famous works include Adha Gaon (The Divided Village), Topi Shukla, Os ki Boond, Neem ka Ped, Main Tulsi Tere Aangan Ki, Disco Dancer, Karz, Lamhe, Aaina, and Mahabharat. His works vividly depict the agony and turmoil of the consequences of the partition of India, the life in feudal India, and the ordinary happiness, love, pain, and sadness of ordinary people. He also participated in the Indian independence movement and supported Gandhi’s non-violence. He won the Filmfare Best Dialogue Award for the film Main Tulsi Tere Aangan Ki in 1979

Raza died on 15 March 1992 in Mumbai at the age of 64. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest literary figures of India and the world. His works have been translated into many languages and have inspired generations of readers and writers. He received many awards and honors for his literary contributions, such as the Sahitya Akademi Award, the Padma Shri, and the Bharat Ratna (posthumously)
