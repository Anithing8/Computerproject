Harivansh Rai Bachchan

Harivansh Rai Bachchan was a famous Hindi poet and writer who was part of the Nayi Kavita literary movement of the early 20th century. He was also the father of Bollywood superstar Amitabh Bachchan and the grandfather of Abhishek Bachchan and Shweta Bachchan Nanda

Bachchan was born on 27 November 1907 in Babupatti, Uttar Pradesh, India. He belonged to a Kayastha family and used the pen name "Bachchan" instead of Srivastava. He taught at Allahabad University and worked at All India Radio and the Ministry of External Affairs. He married twice and had two sons, Amitabh and Ajitabh

Bachchan is best known for his poetry collections such as Madhushala, Madhubala, Madhukalash, Nisha Nimantran, Ekaant Sangeet, Aakul Antar, and Satarangini. He also wrote autobiographies, novels, plays, essays, and songs. His poems reflect his romantic, humanistic, patriotic, and social themes. He was influenced by Hindi, Awadhi, Persian, and Urdu languages and literature. He received many awards and honors such as the Sahitya Akademi Award, the Padma Bhushan, and the Saraswati Samman. He died on 18 January 2003 in Mumbai at the age of 95
