Amrita Pritam

Amrita Pritam was an Indian poet, writer, and lyricist who wrote in Punjabi and Hindi. She was a prominent figure in Punjabi literature and the first woman to win the Sahitya Akademi Award in 1956. She wrote over 100 books of poetry, fiction, biographies, essays, and songs that reflect her versatile genius and humanistic vision. She is best remembered for her poem Ajj aakhaan Waris Shah nu (Today I invoke Waris Shah), an expression of her anguish over the partition of India

Amrita Pritam was born as Amrit Kaur on 31 August 1919 in Gujranwala, Punjab, in British India. She was the only child of Raj Bibi, a school teacher, and Kartar Singh Hitkari, a poet, scholar, and editor. She lost her mother at the age of 11 and her father raised her in Lahore. She started writing poetry at a young age and published her first collection under the pen name Bhanusimha (Sun Lion) when she was 16. When India was partitioned into India and Pakistan in 1947, she migrated from Lahore to India. She witnessed the horrors of communal violence and expressed her grief and pain in her poems. She also wrote about the social issues such as caste discrimination, child marriage, dowry, corruption, exploitation, and injustice. She also depicted the role of women in society and advocated for their education and empowerment. She used simple and colloquial language that appealed to the masses. She was influenced by Hindi, Urdu, Persian, Sanskrit, and English languages and literature

Some of her famous works are Pinjar (The Skeleton), a novel about the violence against women during the partition; Ajj aakhaan Waris Shah nu (Today I invoke Waris Shah), a poem that invokes the 18th-century Punjabi poet Waris Shah to speak out against the atrocities; Sunehade (Messages), a collection of poems that won her the Sahitya Akademi Award; Rasidi Ticket (The Revenue Stamp), her autobiography that reveals her personal life and relationships; Main Tulsi Tere Aangan Ki (I am Tulsi in Your Courtyard), a song that won her the Filmfare Best Lyricist Award; and Mahabharat, a TV serial for which she wrote the script and dialogues based on the epic of the same name

Amrita Pritam died on 31 October 2005 in Delhi at the age of 86. She was honoured with many awards and honors for her literary contributions, such as the Padma Shri, the Bharatiya Jnanpith, the Padma Vibhushan, and the Sahitya Akademi Fellowship. She is widely regarded as one of the greatest literary figures of India and the world. Her works have been translated into many languages and have inspired generations of readers and writers
